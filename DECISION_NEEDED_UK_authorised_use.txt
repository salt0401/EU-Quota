DECISION NEEDED — UK Category 1 "authorised use" quotas (discuss Friday)
=========================================================================
Date raised: 6 July 2026
Deadline context: quota update must be ready and tested by 17 July 2026.

THE QUESTION
------------
Should the MEPS report track the UK Category 1 authorised-use quotas?
They are currently INCLUDED (3 extra rows on the United Kingdom sheet).
This goes slightly beyond the brief, which scoped the UK update to
"quota volumes and order numbers ... in Tables 3 and 4" of the DBT notice.

THE FACTS
---------
- The DBT notice "UK's steel trade measure from 1 July 2026" opens, in
  addition to the ordinary Table 3/4 quotas, a separate authorised-use
  quota for Category 1 (hot-rolled sheet/strip imported for downstream
  processing) — Table 1 of the notice: 595,950 t in Q1 with a 40%
  per-country per-quarter cap.
- That is roughly FIVE TIMES the combined ordinary Category 1 quotas
  (116,750 t/quarter), so it is most of the true UK hot-rolled headroom.
- Its order numbers are NOT in Table 4; they are only published on the
  UK Integrated Online Tariff. Verified live on the HMRC quota API
  (2026-07-06):
      058673  European Union   238,380 t/quarter   (6,030 t already drawn)
      058674  India            238,380 t/quarter
      058675  Residual         119,190 t/quarter
      (058672 returns no data — unused)
- They are scraped through the exact same API as every other UK quota;
  no code changes were needed.

CURRENT STATE
-------------
- data/input/uk_quota_urls.xlsx contains the 3 rows (75 UK rows total),
  category label: "Non-alloy and other alloy hot-rolled sheets and strips
  (authorised use) - 1".
- UK_QUOTA_ORDER_NUMBERS in src/uk_scraper.py includes them
  ('1_Authorised_Use_*' keys).
- The customer template's UK analyst note mentions the authorised-use
  quota exists.

TO KEEP (no action needed)
--------------------------
Nothing to do. Customers importing hot-rolled coil for processing see
the full picture.

TO REMOVE (if MEPS wants strict Table 3/4 scope)
------------------------------------------------
1. Delete the 3 rows (order numbers 058673-058675) from
   data/input/uk_quota_urls.xlsx.
2. Delete the three '1_Authorised_Use_*' entries in
   src/uk_scraper.py UK_QUOTA_ORDER_NUMBERS.
3. In tests/test_uk_scraper.py, revert test_expected_order_number_set
   to expect 72 numbers = range(058600..058671).
4. Optionally reword the United Kingdom sheet analyst note (template) to
   say authorised-use usage is NOT included in this report.
